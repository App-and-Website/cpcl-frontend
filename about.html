About CPCL Journey

The Centre for Puja and Culture Ltd (CPCL) The concept of The Centre for Puja and Culture Ltd (CPCL) was founded in 2003, inspired by the vision and values of its parent organisation, the Bangladesh Society for Puja & Culture Inc (BSPC). From the very beginning, CPCL was more than just a cultural initiative; it was a dream to create a spiritual and cultural sanctuary for the community. We work together:

  • to put the community first;
  • to respect people & their views;
  • to care for others;
  • to excel in ethical actions.

CPCL Objectives

  • Build and maintain facilities for the members of the Company to perform religious and cultural festivals, and promote education through social construction;
  • Have a permanent place of worship and a place of get-together for the members of the Company;
  • Undertake projects pertaining to learning of Hindu philosophy and Bengali language, and oriental art and culture;
  • Provide facilities for members of the Company to organise ceremonies and Hindu religious services and rituals in special family events, including but not limited to birth, marriage and death;
  • Initiate and run community welfare programs, including skills development, training and settlement for new migrants, support in need for overseas students and temporary residents;
  • Establish and run foundations to perform charitable and social welfare works for the community at large;
  • Promote harmony and mutual respect for other communities living in the Australian multicultural society, through development of understanding, cooperation and tolerance.
